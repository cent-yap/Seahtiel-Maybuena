Childhood

1I was born in a small town in Dinagat Island, Surigao Del Norte where life was simple and peaceful. I came from a big family, people are sometimes scared to mess up with the our family because my grandfather’s brothers was a governor and a vice mayor at that time. My father was a General Director and my uncles were police too so I never really experienced being bullied because of that. I don't have a single memory with my parents being together when I was a kid because they separated when I was 3. My mom left for Singapore so I was left with my dad and two stepbrothers in our house in Dinagat. I was also upset with my mom for a few years because my dad told me that she left me but the truth is that she left because she couldn't handle the way my dad treated her anymore. When mom left, my dad had multiple relationships, his girlfriends were nice, it was just him, he is the problem. He doesn’t get contented so that’s why it ruined his career and reputation. I call my stepmoms mom too. The one that impressed me the most was my stepmom who is from Davao, she stayed with us first and we moved with her to Davao. They have a good life and huge house in Davao. I studied preschool in there while my dad was away working. She treated me as her own daughter and I never felt like she's just my stepmom. She gave me a good life. Not until my dad came back and they got separated, my stepmom was left with no choice and I never saw her again. After that, our father took us to Surigao City, where we stayed with my stepsisters, yes, stepsisters. I have all kinds of steps I guess. Again, my dad left after he brought me and my stepbrother to my stepsister's house. Due to my sisters having many kids, they told us that we need to go to our grandparents so that we can be taken care of properly. That's where I grew up and studied from 1st grade to 4th grade. I have a very chaotic childhood in which I stayed with different relatives but not with my parents. My grandmother took care of me for several years in elementary school. When I was still young, my aunts and uncles had high expectations for me, always imprinting on my brain that I should not lose the honor list. Every time there is a recognition, they expect me to receive many awards and get the first honor, which I always get back then. Life there is simple and slow-paced. There are only three of us living in my grandparents' big house. I never got to see my brother again after her mother took him and returned to Surigao City. I learned to be independent there. Unlike other children, their parents were there to guide them and help them with their homework and schoolworks, I do all my schoolworks and assignments alone without anyone's help, because I have no choice. After a few years, mom came back to see me, I didn't recognize her at first because I didn’t remember her face when she left and I have no memory with her. He came home three times a year or I go to Manila or Singapore for vacation, until when I was in 5th grade when mom worked here and I was in vacation here, my mom and I decided to not go back in Surigao, from then I barely talk to my family on my dad's side because mom doesn't want me to because she has a grudge against my father’s other siblings. I finally met my stepdad that time, I didn't liked him at first but eventually I did because I noticed his efforts for us to become closed. Now, If I have to choose between him and my sperm donor, I’d choose him. He loves me as his own daughter and he loves my mom so much. It’s been 16 years since I saw my biological dad and I don’t have plan on seeing him again.

Teenage

1As I entered my teenage years, High school was a mix of excitement and pressure. The carefree days of childhood were replaced with responsibilities, expectations, and the emotional rollercoaster that came with growing up. High school was both thrilling and challenging. I was no longer just a child playing outside and trying to escape the afternoon naps. I was a student expected to excel, a friend trying to navigate social circles, and a daughter carrying the hopes of his parents. I dedicated myself to my studies, knowing that education was my key to a better future. Late nights were often spent with schoolworks submissions, and ensuring deadlines were met, dance practice here, dance practice there. I also became active in extracurricular activities, joining the debate team and student council, which honed my leadership skills. I became part of the school newspaper, where I discovered my deep passion for writing. I also discovered my passion for acting, public speaking and joined drama club and journalism which I think I lost the passion. However, teenage life was not just about academics. It was also a time of self-discovery, there were moments of rebellion, and lessons learned the hard way, times when I questioned authority and sought independence. Yet, deep down, I knew my parents only wanted what was best for me. Friendships evolved, and I learned that not everyone who walked into my life was meant to stay. I experienced betrayal, misunderstandings, and the sting of losing people I once considered my closest friends. Yet, I also found friendships that would last a lifetime, built on trust, shared dreams, and countless late-night conversations about life and the future. Love, or what I thought was love at the time, also made its way into my teenage years. I felt the excitement of first crushes, the nervousness of sending a handwritten letter, and the joy of stolen glances in the school hallway. Despite these challenges, I remained focused on my dreams, determined to make my parents proud which I did. I graduated with “Highest honor” and was a consistent scholar in Pasig for 8 years but now I lost it. Sucks.

College

1College was a mix of exhilarating highs and crushing lows. Got heartbroken not because of a boy but because I didn’t get to study in my dream school and nothing hurts more than not being able to take your dream course because you chose the course your parents wanted. It hurts to see people studying my dream course, having a hard time but they’re enjoying it and I’d rather have a hard time doing what I love than having a hard time doing I’m not even good at. It hurts that you are studying but you’re not really enjoying it, it’s not your passion, It’s not what you want. I always tell myself that I can do so much better if I get to study my dream course, the course I’m really passionate about, the course that I know I’d excel. Maybe in another life I’d get to pursue my dream and do what makes me happy. It sucks being an only child because you have to continue your parents’ unfulfilled dreams. College humbled me in so many ways. The subjects were tougher, the professors stricter, and the expectations higher. I pulled countless all-nighters, studying until the first light of dawn and still didn’t get the score I expected to have. There were times I cried because of how hard I try to study the subject I still couldn’t comprehend. One of the saddest encounters in college is seeing yourself from excited learner to silent sufferer. There were moments when I felt like giving up, when the weight of responsibilities felt unbearable. But I reminded myself of my purpose, of the sacrifices my parents made to send me here, and of the dreams I refused to abandon. Some friendships were fleeting, I experienced being betrayed by a friend that I treated as bestfriend. I also experienced my first taste of independence, not just in managing my own life but in making decisions that would define my future. In college, I fell in love with the most unexpected person at the most unexpected time. I also experienced working for more than a year in a BPO industry just because I wanted to experience working, got promoted but refused due to I know higher position, bigger salary, which means bigger responsibilities. Had to gave up working even though I gained friends and enjoyed it to focus on my studies. Now, I am helping my mom with her business. So thankful for my mom’s hardwork, for everything she does and for giving me a good life. In life, we don't always get what we want; there are things we must accept and face, even if we're not happy, as long as the people around us are.
